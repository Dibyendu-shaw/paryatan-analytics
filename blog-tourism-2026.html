Tourism 2026: More Heart, Better Data

DS
Dibyendu Shaw
Founder, Paryatan Analytics · Kolkata
📅 2025  ·  ⏱️ 4 min read  ·  🏷️ Tourism · Data · Hospitality

If you've ever worked a double shift in a hotel or managed a tour during peak season, you know that "hospitality" is often just organized chaos.

We guess when the next rush will come. We guess which room rate will "feel" right for the weekend. We guess which menu item is actually making us money.

Our hearts are in the right place, but our math is often missing.

When I transitioned from the hospitality floor to building Paryatan Analytics, I realized something profound. I was reading about the National Tourism Policy 2026 updates this week. The government is pushing hard on "Data-Driven Tourism" and upskilling through Mission Karmayogi.

It's a signal to all of us — the Indian traveler isn't just looking for a bed anymore. They're looking for a story. Whether it's a quiet retreat in the hills or a deep dive into Kolkata's heritage, they want experiences that feel personal.

If we don't use data to understand these shifts, we aren't just losing numbers — we're losing the chance to serve our guests better.

The biggest lesson I've learned isn't about complex AI. It's about clarity. It's about turning a messy spreadsheet into a simple dashboard that says, "Hey, look here — this is where your business is actually growing."

That's what Paryatan Analytics is all about. Not fancy algorithms or complex models — just clear, honest insights that help India's tourism and hospitality businesses make better decisions every day.

Are you still running your business on a "hunch," or are you ready to see what your data is trying to tell you?
